Frequently Asked Questions

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) code is a 6-digit uniform code that classifies goods and services for taxation purposes under GST. It helps in standardizing the classification of goods across the country and ensures uniform taxation.

GST rates are determined by the GST Council based on various factors including the nature of the product/service, its essentiality, luxury status, and previous tax incidence. The council aims to keep essential items at lower rates and luxury/sin goods at higher rates.

Yes, GST rates can change based on decisions made by the GST Council. The council meets periodically to review and revise rates based on industry feedback, economic conditions, and government policy. Any changes are notified through official government channels.

CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST) are collected on intra-state transactions, with each being half of the total GST rate. IGST (Integrated GST) is collected on inter-state transactions and is equal to the total GST rate. For example, for a product with 18% GST, intra-state transactions would have 9% CGST and 9% SGST, while inter-state transactions would have 18% IGST.

To find the correct GST rate, you need to identify the HSN code for your product or service. You can use the search function on this page, refer to the official GST rate finder on the GST Portal, or consult with a tax professional. Once you know the HSN code, you can look up the corresponding GST rate in the rate tables.
