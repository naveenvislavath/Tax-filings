Classification Based on Investment & Turnover

Who Can Apply

  • Any business entity - Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, Private Limited Company, etc.
  • Manufacturing or service sector businesses operating in India
  • Both existing businesses and new startups
  • No minimum operational period requirement

Required Documents

  • Identity Proof

    Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, or Passport of the business owner/partners/directors

  • Address Proof

    Electricity Bill, Rent Agreement, or Property Deed of business premises

  • Business Registration Documents

    Certificate of Incorporation, Partnership Deed, Shop & Establishment Certificate, etc.

  • Business PAN Card

    PAN Card of the business entity

  • Bank Account Details

    Bank account details of the business with canceled cheque

  • Business Details

    Information about investment in plant & machinery/equipment and annual turnover

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about MSME registration

MSME registration is the official recognition of your business as a Micro, Small, or Medium Enterprise by the Government of India. It's important because it provides access to various benefits including priority sector lending, subsidies, tax benefits, and preference in government tenders. These benefits help small businesses grow, improve competitiveness, and contribute to economic development.

No, MSME registration is not mandatory for all businesses. However, it is highly recommended as it provides numerous benefits and advantages. Without registration, businesses cannot access the special schemes, subsidies, and support measures designed for MSMEs by the government.

Udyam Registration is the current official process for MSME registration in India. It replaced the earlier system called Udyog Aadhaar Memorandum (UAM) in July 2020. So essentially, Udyam Registration is the new name for MSME registration. The new system is completely online, paperless, and based on self-declaration. It's linked with your PAN and GST details for verification.

The MSME registration process through the Udyam portal typically takes 3-5 working days from the date of application submission. However, this timeline can vary based on the accuracy of information provided, document completeness, and verification process. With GrowthLink's assistance, we ensure all details are correctly submitted to avoid any delays in processing.

Yes, you can register an existing business for MSME certification at any time, provided it meets the eligibility criteria based on investment in plant & machinery/equipment and turnover. There is no restriction on the age of the business for MSME registration. In fact, many established businesses register to take advantage of the benefits offered to MSMEs.

Under the new Udyam Registration system, there is no requirement for renewal of the MSME certificate. The registration is valid for a lifetime. However, if there are any changes in your business details such as name, address, bank details, or if your business grows beyond the MSME category limits, you are required to update this information on the Udyam portal.

Yes, GrowthLink provides comprehensive post-registration support to help you maximize the benefits of your MSME certification. Our experts can guide you on accessing priority sector lending, applying for subsidies, participating in government tenders, and utilizing various schemes specifically designed for MSMEs. This support is included in our Standard and Premium packages, and can also be availed separately.

If your business grows beyond the defined limits for MSME (investment exceeding ₹50 crore and turnover exceeding ₹250 crore), you should update this information on the Udyam portal. While you will no longer be classified as an MSME, your registration doesn't become invalid. However, you may no longer be eligible for certain MSME-specific benefits. The government provides a 3-year transition period where you can still avail some benefits even after outgrowing the MSME category.
