Form DIR-3 KYC

Annual KYC for all directors

Due: 30 June 2025

Every director who has been allotted a Director Identification Number (DIN) must file Form DIR-3 KYC annually to verify their details. Failure to file will result in the DIN being marked as 'Deactivated' with a penalty for reactivation.

Form AOC-4

Financial Statement Filing

Due: 30 October 2025

Companies must file their financial statements including balance sheet, profit and loss account, and board's report within 30 days of the Annual General Meeting (AGM). For specified companies, XBRL format is mandatory.

Form MGT-7

Annual Return Filing

Due: 29 November 2025

Every company must file its annual return within 60 days of the Annual General Meeting (AGM). The return includes details about registered office, business activities, shares, debentures, indebtedness, members, directors, and more.

Annual General Meeting (AGM)

Mandatory Annual Meeting

Due: 30 September 2025

Every company must hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) within 6 months from the end of the financial year. The first AGM must be held within 9 months from the end of the first financial year.

Form MSME-1

MSME Payment Compliance

Due: Half-yearly (30 Oct & 30 Apr)

Companies must report outstanding payments to Micro and Small Enterprises if they exceed 45 days. This form must be filed half-yearly by all companies who have outstanding payments to MSMEs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about ROC compliance

ROC (Registrar of Companies) compliance refers to the statutory requirements that companies in India must fulfill under the Companies Act, 2013. This includes filing various forms, maintaining statutory registers, conducting annual general meetings, and ensuring directors meet their KYC obligations. Compliance is mandatory for all companies registered under the Companies Act.

Missing ROC filing deadlines can result in significant penalties. The Companies Act imposes additional fees for late filings, which increase with the duration of the delay. For serious non-compliance, directors may face disqualification, and the company could be struck off the register. In some cases, criminal proceedings may also be initiated against the company and its officers.

Yes, Director KYC (Know Your Customer) is mandatory every year. As per the Companies Act, every individual who has been allotted a Director Identification Number (DIN) must submit Form DIR-3 KYC annually to verify their details. Failure to file this form by the deadline (usually September 30) results in the DIN being marked as 'Deactivated', and a penalty fee is required for reactivation.

Companies must maintain several statutory registers as per the Companies Act, including: Register of Members, Register of Directors and Key Managerial Personnel, Register of Loans and Investments, Register of Charges, Minutes Book for Board and General Meetings, Register of Contracts with related parties, and Register of Renewed and Duplicate Share Certificates. These registers must be kept at the registered office and be available for inspection.

While it's technically possible to handle ROC compliance yourself, it requires in-depth knowledge of the Companies Act and regular updates on changing regulations. The process can be time-consuming and complex, with severe penalties for errors or omissions. Most businesses find it more cost-effective to engage professional services like GrowthLink to ensure complete and timely compliance, allowing them to focus on their core business activities.
