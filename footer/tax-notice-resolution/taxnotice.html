Tax Notices We Handle

We provide expert resolution services for all types of tax notices issued by the Income Tax Department of India

Section 143(1) Intimation

Initial assessment notice indicating discrepancies between your return and the department's calculations.

  • Mismatch in tax calculation
  • Incorrect TDS claims
  • Deduction discrepancies

Section 143(2) Scrutiny

Detailed examination notice requiring explanation and documentation for specific items in your return.

  • High-value transactions
  • Significant deductions claimed
  • Income source verification

Section 156 Demand Notice

Notice for tax payment due based on assessment, requiring immediate attention to avoid penalties.

  • Outstanding tax demands
  • Penalty charges
  • Interest calculations

Section 139(9) Defective Return

Notice indicating errors or omissions in your filed return that need correction.

  • Missing schedules or forms
  • Incorrect personal information
  • Unsigned or incomplete returns

Section 245 Refund Adjustment

Notice regarding adjustment of refund against outstanding tax demands from previous years.

  • Refund adjustments
  • Prior year demands
  • Disputed tax amounts

Section 148 Reassessment

Notice for reopening previously filed and assessed returns due to suspected income escapement.

  • Unreported income
  • Foreign assets disclosure
  • High-value transactions

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about tax notices and our resolution services

If you receive a tax notice, don't panic. First, check the notice type and due date for response. Make sure to read it carefully to understand what the tax department is asking for. Then, contact our tax experts immediately by uploading your notice through our secure portal or sending it via email/WhatsApp. Our team will analyze the notice and guide you through the resolution process.

The resolution time varies depending on the type and complexity of the notice. Simple notices like Section 143(1) intimations can typically be resolved within 1-2 weeks. More complex notices like scrutiny under Section 143(2) may take 1-3 months due to the detailed documentation and potential hearings involved. Our team works efficiently to resolve notices as quickly as possible while ensuring thorough responses.

The required documents depend on the notice type. Generally, you'll need to provide the original tax notice, your PAN card, the income tax return for the relevant assessment year, and any supporting documents mentioned in the notice (such as investment proofs, bank statements, TDS certificates, etc.). Our team will provide a specific checklist based on your notice type after the initial assessment.

Yes, our Premium Resolution package includes representation in hearings with tax authorities. Our experienced tax consultants and CAs can attend hearings on your behalf with proper authorization. They will present your case, respond to queries, and negotiate with tax officers to achieve the best possible outcome. For Standard and Basic packages, representation can be added as an additional service if required.

If you've missed a deadline, it's still important to respond as soon as possible. In many cases, we can file a condonation of delay request explaining the reasons for the late response. Our team has experience in handling such situations and can often still achieve positive outcomes. However, it's always best to address notices promptly to avoid penalties or adverse assessments.

While we cannot guarantee specific outcomes as final decisions rest with tax authorities, we do guarantee our best professional efforts to achieve the most favorable resolution possible. Our success rate is over 95% in significantly reducing or eliminating tax demands. If we believe a notice cannot be favorably resolved based on the facts and applicable laws, we will advise you honestly about the best approach to minimize potential liabilities.
